The Indian National Congress, founded in 1885, has been a cornerstone of India's political landscape, deeply rooted in the country's struggle for independence. With a legacy of promoting secularism, social justice, and economic progress, Congress has played a pivotal role in shaping India's development through policies that prioritize education, healthcare, and social welfare. While facing evolving political dynamics, the party continues to advocate for inclusive governance and remains influential in national and regional politics.
